Our History

St. Patrick's Catholic Church was founded in 1965 by a small group of dedicated Catholics who envisioned a place where faith, community, and service would flourish together. What began as a modest congregation meeting in a local community center has grown into a vibrant parish that serves hundreds of families in our community.

Our church building, completed in 1968, stands as a testament to the dedication and generosity of our founding members. The beautiful stained glass windows, handcrafted altar, and peaceful prayer garden have been lovingly maintained and enhanced over the decades, creating a sacred space that inspires worship and reflection.

Throughout our history, St. Patrick's has been blessed with exceptional pastoral leadership and committed lay volunteers who have helped shape our parish into the welcoming community it is today. We have weathered challenges, celebrated milestones, and consistently maintained our commitment to serving both our parish family and the broader community.

Our Mission

St. Patrick's Catholic Church exists to glorify God by making disciples of Jesus Christ through worship, fellowship, spiritual growth, and service to others. We are committed to creating an environment where all people can encounter the love of Christ, grow in their faith, and discover their purpose in God's kingdom.

Our mission is lived out through:

  • Authentic Worship: Celebrating the Mass and sacraments with reverence, beauty, and participation
  • Biblical Teaching: Proclaiming God's Word with clarity and relevance for daily life
  • Spiritual Formation: Providing opportunities for growth in prayer, study, and discipleship
  • Christian Fellowship: Building meaningful relationships within our parish family
  • Compassionate Service: Reaching out to those in need both locally and globally
  • Evangelization: Sharing the Good News of Jesus Christ with our community

Our Core Values

Faith

We believe in the power of faith to transform lives and communities. Our foundation is built on Scripture, Tradition, and the teachings of the Catholic Church, guided by the Holy Spirit in all we do.

Community

We are called to be one body in Christ, supporting and caring for one another. Our parish is a place where all people are welcomed, valued, and encouraged to participate in the life of our church family.

Service

Following Christ's example, we are committed to serving others, especially the poor, marginalized, and vulnerable. We believe that service is an expression of our love for God and neighbor.

Excellence

We strive for excellence in all aspects of our ministry, from our worship and programs to our facilities and administration, always seeking to honor God with our best efforts.

Growth

We are committed to continuous spiritual growth and learning, both individually and as a community, always seeking to deepen our relationship with God and understanding of His will.

Parish Life Today

Today, St. Patrick's is home to over 800 registered families and continues to grow. Our diverse community includes longtime members whose families have been part of our parish for generations, as well as newcomers who have recently discovered our welcoming church family.

We offer a full range of ministries and programs for all ages, from our vibrant children's religious education program to our active senior ministry. Our parish school serves students from kindergarten through eighth grade, providing excellent academic education rooted in Catholic values.

St. Patrick's is proud to be actively involved in our local community through various outreach programs, including our food pantry, homeless shelter ministry, and partnership with local charitable organizations. We believe that faith in action makes a real difference in the world around us.

Our Pastoral Team

Father Michael O'Brien

Pastor

Father Michael has served as pastor of St. Patrick's since 2018. Ordained in 2005, he brings a passion for preaching, pastoral care, and community outreach. He holds a Master's in Divinity from St. Mary's Seminary and enjoys hiking and reading in his free time.

Father James Rodriguez

Associate Pastor

Father James joined our parish in 2021. He specializes in youth ministry and faith formation. Ordained in 2015, he has a gift for connecting with young people and helping them grow in their faith journey. He is fluent in Spanish and English.

Deacon Robert Thompson

Permanent Deacon

Deacon Bob was ordained in 2010 and has been serving at St. Patrick's since 2012. He assists with liturgical duties and leads our social justice initiatives. A retired teacher, he brings decades of educational experience to our religious education programs.

Sarah Mitchell

Director of Music Ministry

Sarah has been leading our music ministry since 2016. With a Master's in Sacred Music, she directs our adult choir, children's choir, and contemporary music group. Her beautiful voice and musical leadership enhance our worship experience.

Mary Catherine Davis

Parish Manager

Mary Catherine oversees the day-to-day operations of our parish. With over 15 years of experience in parish administration, she ensures everything runs smoothly and helps coordinate our various ministries and programs.

Patricia Johnson

Director of Religious Education

Patricia leads our comprehensive religious education program for children and adults. With a Master's in Theology and 20 years of teaching experience, she helps people of all ages grow in their understanding and love of the Catholic faith.
