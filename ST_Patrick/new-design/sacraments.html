The Seven Sacraments

Encounter Christ through the sacred signs of God's grace

What Are Sacraments?

The sacraments are efficacious signs of grace, instituted by Christ and entrusted to the Church, by which divine life is dispensed to us. They are visible rites recognized by the Church as being instituted by Jesus Christ to confer the grace they signify. Through the sacraments, we encounter Christ in a real and tangible way, receiving His grace for our spiritual journey.

Holy Orders

Holy Orders is the sacrament through which men are ordained as bishops, priests, or deacons to serve the Church. They receive a special grace to act in the person of Christ, serving His people.

Who: Men called to priesthood/diaconate
Discernment: Contact your diocesan vocations office

Matrimony (Marriage)

The Sacrament of Matrimony unites a baptized man and woman in a lifelong covenant of faithful love. Through this sacrament, spouses help each other attain holiness and welcome and educate children.

Preparation: 6 months advance notice required
