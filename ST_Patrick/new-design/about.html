Our History

A Journey of Faith Since 1932

The Foundation of Faith

The history of St. Patrick's Catholic Church, Igbogila ‚Äì Ipaja dates back to the late 1920s, rooted in a profound story of faith, healing, and generosity.

The Beginning

Pa Akanbi Akiniyi, the patriarch of the Akiniyi Royal family, donated a parcel of land from his farmland in Igbogila ‚Äì Ipaja to the Catholic mission. Originally a member of the United Native African Church, Pa Akiniyi was converted to the Catholic faith following the healing of his son, Sir James Fadipe Akiniyi, by the prayers of St. Anthony prayer Group in the late 1920s.

In gratitude for this miracle, Pa Akanbi Akiniyi promised to donate land from his extensive farmland to the Catholic mission. Though he did not demarcate the land before his death, his son, James Fadipe Akiniyi, faithfully actualized his father's promise.

The first mass of the church was celebrated by Rev. Fr. Koechan of the Catholic Church, Iteri Abeokuta, in Pa Akanbi Akiniyi's house at Ipaja. This sacred beginning established a foundation that would grow into the vibrant parish we know today.

Our Journey Through Time

Key milestones in the history of St. Patrick's Catholic Church

1920s - 1930s: The Foundation
Late 1920s

Miraculous Healing & Conversion

Pa Akanbi Akiniyi's son, Sir James Fadipe Akiniyi, was healed through the prayers of St. Anthony prayer Group, leading to Pa Akiniyi's conversion from United Native African Church to Catholicism.

1932

First Physical Structure

The first church building, made of bamboo, was erected at the spot of the present Rev. Father's house. The church was handed over to Rev. Fr. Dealey of St. Paul's Catholic Church, Ebute-Metta.

1940s: Growth & Development
1940

Church Renovation

St. Patrick's Church building was renovated with mould blocks by Very Rev. Monsgr. Julius Oni of the Catholic Church, Ota, Ogun State.

1970s: Modern Foundations
1978

Modern Church Building Foundation

Foundation for the modern church building was laid by Rev. Fr. John Hannon using cement blocks and iron, marking a new era of development.

2000s: Parish Elevation
Nov 27, 2005

Elevated to Parish Status

St. Patrick's Catholic Church was elevated to parish status by His Eminence, Cardinal Anthony Okogie, transitioning from a station of St. Ferdinand Catholic Church.

2010s: Expansion & Modernization
May 29, 2012

Parish Office Complex

The Parish office complex was blessed and opened, providing administrative facilities for the growing parish.

May 19, 2013

Pentecost Celebration

Colorful Pentecost Feast Day Celebration in conjunction with the Holy Ghost Congregation, South West Province, Nigeria (Lagos Zone).

April 6, 2015

Foundation of Ultra-Modern Church

Foundation laying stone of the new ultra-modern church building was laid by His Grace, Most Rev. Alfred Adewale Martins, Archbishop of Lagos.

March 28, 2016

10th Anniversary

St. Patrick's celebrated its Feast Day and 10 years Anniversary of the creation of the Parish with great joy and thanksgiving.

Nov 24, 2018

Church Dedication

The Solemn Dedication of the new ultra-modern church building was held, marking a historic milestone in the parish's journey. To the greater glory of God, the building stands as a testament to faith and community.

2020s: Continued Growth
2024

Third Quasi-Parish

Catholic Church of the Archangels, Bada, was pronounced a quasi-parish, marking the third outstation nurtured by St. Patrick's to reach parish status (joining St. Pius Ayobo and Church of the Annunciation Ishefu).
