Holy Communion

The Source and Summit of Christian Life

What is the Holy Eucharist?

The Holy Eucharist, also known as Holy Communion or the Blessed Sacrament, is the true Body and Blood, Soul and Divinity of our Lord Jesus Christ under the appearances of bread and wine. It is the source and summit of the Christian life, the greatest of all the sacraments.

"I am the living bread that came down from heaven. Whoever eats this bread will live forever. This bread is my flesh, which I will give for the life of the world." - John 6:51

The Last Supper

At the Last Supper, Jesus took bread, blessed it, broke it, and gave it to His disciples saying, "Take and eat; this is my body." Then He took the cup of wine and said, "Drink from it, all of you. This is my blood of the covenant, which is poured out for many for the forgiveness of sins." He commanded them to "Do this in memory of me."

First Holy Communion Preparation

First Holy Communion is a significant milestone in a Catholic child's faith journey. Our parish offers comprehensive preparation to help children understand and appreciate this sacred sacrament.

Requirements:

  • Child must be baptized in the Catholic Church
  • Typically around age 7 or in second grade (age of reason)
  • Must complete required catechism classes
  • Regular attendance at Sunday Mass with family
  • Parents/guardians must attend preparation meetings
  • Child should have received the Sacrament of Reconciliation (First Confession)

Preparation Program Includes:

  • Weekly religious education classes
  • Understanding of the Real Presence of Christ in the Eucharist
  • Learning proper reverence and respect for the Blessed Sacrament
  • Practice of prayers and responses for Mass
  • Retreat day before First Communion
  • Rehearsal for the ceremony

Receiving Holy Communion

Who Can Receive?

Baptized Catholics who have received First Holy Communion and are in a state of grace (free from mortal sin) may receive the Eucharist.

Preparation Before Receiving:

  • Fast for at least one hour before receiving (water and medicine are permitted)
  • If conscious of mortal sin, receive the Sacrament of Reconciliation before Communion
  • Approach with reverence, faith, and devotion

How to Receive:

You may receive Holy Communion on the tongue or in the hand. If receiving in the hand, extend both hands, one on top of the other, with palms up. The minister will place the Host in your hand, and you should step aside and reverently consume it immediately.
