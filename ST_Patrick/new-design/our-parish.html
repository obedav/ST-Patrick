OUR Parish

A Vibrant Community of Faith

Welcome to St. Patrick's Parish

St. Patrick's Catholic Church, Igbogila, Ipaja is a vibrant parish community that has been serving God's people since 1932. We are a diverse family of believers united in our faith, committed to worship, service, and the proclamation of the Gospel of Jesus Christ.

Our Mission

To be a welcoming community that proclaims the Gospel, celebrates the sacraments, and serves others in the spirit of Jesus Christ, fostering spiritual growth and unity among all God's people.

Parish Structure

Pastoral Leadership

Our parish is shepherded by the Congregation of the Holy Spirit (Spiritans), led by our Parish Priest, Rev. Fr. Agbapajuru Cyriacus CSSp, together with associate priests and deacons who serve our community with dedication.

Parish Pastoral Council

The Parish Pastoral Council works collaboratively with the parish priest to plan, coordinate, and oversee pastoral activities, ensuring the spiritual and temporal welfare of the parish.

Parish Finance Council

The Finance Council assists in the stewardship of parish resources, ensuring transparency and accountability in financial matters.

Parish Milestones

  • 1932: First church building erected
  • November 27, 2005: Elevated to parish status by Cardinal Anthony Okogie
  • April 6, 2015: Foundation stone laid for ultra-modern church by Archbishop Alfred Martins
  • November 24, 2018: Solemn dedication of the new church building
  • 2024: Catholic Church of the Archangels, Bada, pronounced a quasi-parish - our third outstation to achieve this status

Parish Laity Council (PLC)

The Parish Laity Council is charged by the Decree on the Apostolate of the Laity (Apostolicam Actuositatem) with the goal of intensifying the apostolic activity of the laity by promoting the apostolate and coordinating parish lay initiatives. While individual groups are responsible for organizing and directing their own activities, the parish laity council is charged with the coordination and promotion of lay apostolic zeal amongst parishioners.
